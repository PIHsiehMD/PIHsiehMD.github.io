Profile

From bedside nursing to research-driven medicine.

I'm a post-baccalaureate medical student at National Chung Hsing University, building on a Bachelor of Science in Nursing and a Master of Science in Public Health, specializing in Preventive Medicine, both from National Taiwan University.

My research bridges clinical care and population health, spanning geriatric epidemiology, sarcopenia, cognitive aging, and multimorbidity, alongside applying natural language processing to suicide-prevention surveillance. I work fluently with SAS, R, and SPSS to turn longitudinal cohort data into clinical insight.

I'm currently completing clinical rotations as a medical clerk across teaching hospitals in central Taiwan. My recent work reaches into pediatric orthopaedics and medical-device innovation, including an award-winning translucent hip brace for newborns.
